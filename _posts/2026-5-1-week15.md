It's a bit bittersweet to make this post. I really did have a very enjoyable time in this course,
and to see it come to an end is something that is both triumphant, while also being slightly sad.
Now that I am properly informed about open-source, alongside how to be a meaningful contributor, 
I can continue to contribute and learn, but it won't ever be the same as the first time I dipped my
toes into the discipline. Regardless, I will still look back on this period of my software development
journey quite fondly for leaving me with this knowledge. On to some other group projects.

### Classmate Presentations
Overall, the work that other students in the class have been creating beyond my personal group via
Keycloak has been conveyed in a way that has been pretty engaging. From productivity workspace apps
like Affine, to open source rewritten optimizations of existing tools like nanobot, everyone has had
very interesting insights, lessons learned, and challenges faced with their respective projects.
I'm eager to see what the final groups hold in their presentations next Monday.
